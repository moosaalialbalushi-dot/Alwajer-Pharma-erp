# FDA 21 CFR 211.42: Design and Construction Features

This reference outlines the key requirements for pharmaceutical facility design as per US FDA regulations.

## Core Requirements

1. **Suitable Size and Construction**: Buildings must be of adequate size and construction to facilitate cleaning, maintenance, and proper operations.
2. **Defined Areas**: Separate or defined areas must be established for:
    - Receipt and storage of components (quarantine, released, rejected).
    - Manufacturing and processing operations.
    - Packaging and labeling operations.
    - Quarantine and storage of finished drug products.
    - Control and laboratory operations.
3. **Flow Control**: Design must prevent contamination and mix-ups through logical flow of materials and personnel.
4. **Environmental Control**: Operations like aseptic processing require specific environmental controls (HEPA filters, pressure differentials).

## Material and Personnel Flow Principles

- **Unidirectional Flow**: Preferred to prevent backtracking and cross-contamination.
- **Airlocks**: Use between areas of different cleanliness classes.
- **Segregation**: Physical or spatial separation of incompatible operations.
