# Brainstorming Principles

Brainstorming is a foundational creative problem-solving technique designed for group collaboration, first conceptualized by Alex Osborn in the 1940s.

## The Core Principle: Deferred Judgment

The most critical element of brainstorming is the intentional separation of **idea creation** from **idea evaluation**. 

### Divergent Thinking
During the generation phase, participants explore many possible solutions without restriction. This free-flowing exchange allows one person's thought to trigger a new idea in another.
- **Quantity is key**: The more ideas, the higher the chance of finding a "gem".
- **No criticism**: Any form of evaluation during this phase shuts down creative thinking.
- **Wild ideas**: Unconventional or "crazy" ideas are encouraged as they often lead to breakthrough solutions.

### Convergent Thinking
Once the generation phase is complete, the focus shifts to organizing, discussing, and refining the most promising concepts into actionable solutions.

## The Three-Step Flow
1. **Generate**: Produce a high volume of ideas without judgment.
2. **Deferred Judgment**: A critical bridge that prevents immediate shutdown of creative thought.
3. **Refine**: Practical evaluation and selection of solutions.
