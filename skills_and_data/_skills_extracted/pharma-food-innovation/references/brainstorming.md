# Brainstorming & Innovation Frameworks

This document outlines various frameworks and techniques for generating and refining new product concepts, solving complex problems, and driving innovation in the pharmaceutical, food, multi-ingredient extraction, and cosmetic industries.

## Foundational Brainstorming

Brainstorming is a foundational creative problem-solving technique designed for group collaboration. The core principle is to generate a high volume of ideas about a specific problem or topic within a non-judgmental environment. This approach intentionally separates idea creation from idea evaluation to prevent premature criticism from stifling creativity. The process encourages divergent thinking, where participants explore many possible solutions without restriction.

| Phase | Description | Key Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Idea Generation** | Focus purely on quantity; wild and unconventional ideas are welcomed. | Defer judgment. |
| **Deferred Judgment** | A critical bridge between generating wild ideas and refining practical solutions. | Pause criticism. |
| **Evaluation & Refinement** | Organize, discuss, and refine the most promising concepts into actionable solutions. | Select and polish. |

## Design Thinking

Design Thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that integrates the needs of people, the possibilities of technology, and the requirements for business success. This iterative, non-linear process is about gaining deep empathy for the end-user. By observing and engaging with people, teams can uncover unmet needs and challenge their own assumptions.

| Phase | Goal |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Empathize** | Develop a thorough understanding of the user's challenges through research. |
| **Define** | Synthesize research into a clear, human-centered problem statement. |
| **Ideate** | Generate a wide range of potential solutions without constraints. |
| **Prototype** | Create low-cost, scaled-down versions of solutions to test concepts. |
| **Test** | Gather feedback from actual users to inform continuous refinement. |

## Structured Innovation Techniques

Several structured techniques provide systematic frameworks for manipulating existing ideas or exploring new ones. These methods are especially powerful for overcoming creative blocks and ensuring a comprehensive analysis of the problem space.

### SCAMPER Technique

The SCAMPER technique uses a checklist of seven prompts to spark innovation by looking at an existing product or service from different angles.

| Prompt | Question to Ask |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Substitute** | What materials, processes, or people can be replaced? |
| **Combine** | Can we join two or more parts of the process or product? |
| **Adapt** | How can we adjust the product to serve a new purpose? |
| **Modify** | What can be magnified, minimized, or changed in quality? |
| **Put to another use** | Can this be used in another industry or by another user? |
| **Eliminate** | What features or steps can be removed to simplify the solution? |
| **Reverse** | What happens if we change the order or orientation? |

### Six Thinking Hats

The Six Thinking Hats technique structures critical thinking by directing focus to one mode of thinking at a time. This alignment minimizes conflict and ensures a comprehensive analysis from multiple perspectives.

| Hat Color | Thinking Mode | Focus Area |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blue** | Process | Managing the agenda, timing, and facilitation. |
| **White** | Facts | Neutral information, data, and identified gaps. |
| **Red** | Emotions | Intuition, feelings, and gut reactions without justification. |
| **Black** | Risks | Critical judgment, caution, and identifying potential flaws. |
| **Yellow** | Benefits | Optimism, value, and identifying potential advantages. |
| **Green** | Creativity | New ideas, possibilities, and unconventional solutions. |

## Advanced Analytical Frameworks

For complex technical or structural problems, advanced frameworks provide logical and repeatable processes for innovation.

### TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)

TRIZ is a systematic, data-driven methodology based on the principle that inventive problems contain fundamental contradictions. Innovation arises from resolving these contradictions using predictable patterns discovered through the analysis of millions of patents.

> "The goal of TRIZ is to resolve a contradiction completely, not find a middle ground. Aim for an ideal final result where the conflict ceases to exist."

### Morphological Analysis

Morphological Analysis is a systematic technique for exploring all possible solutions to a complex problem by breaking it down into its fundamental dimensions. It transforms a daunting, unstructured problem into a manageable set of components organized into a "morphological box."

## Associative and Metaphorical Thinking

These techniques leverage the brain's natural ability to find connections between seemingly unrelated concepts to jolt the mind out of established patterns.

*   **Mind Mapping**: A visual technique that organizes information around a central concept, using keywords, colors, and images to uncover new relationships.
*   **Lateral Thinking**: Approaching problems from unconventional angles through deliberate provocation (e.g., "the cup has no bottom") to disrupt established thought patterns.
*   **Analogical Thinking**: Finding parallels between a current problem and a unrelated situation, such as using biomimicry to solve engineering challenges.
*   **Synectics**: Joining together different and apparently irrelevant elements using metaphors to "make the familiar strange and the strange familiar."
