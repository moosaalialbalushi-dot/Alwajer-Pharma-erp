# Technical Protocols: Extraction & Manufacturing

This document provides detailed technical protocols and technology overviews for the extraction and manufacturing of pharmaceuticals, nutraceuticals, and food processing raw materials.

## 1. Advanced Extraction Technologies

### Supercritical Fluid Extraction (SFE)
- **Application**: High-purity extraction of essential oils, cannabinoids, and bioactive lipids.
- **Protocol**:
  1. Load raw material into the extraction vessel.
  2. Pressurize CO2 to supercritical state (typically >73.8 bar, >31.1°C).
  3. Adjust pressure/temperature to tune solvent selectivity.
  4. Separate extract from CO2 in the collection vessel.
  5. Recirculate CO2 for efficiency.

### Ultrasonic-Assisted Extraction (UAE)
- **Application**: Multi-ingredient extraction from plant matrices.
- **Protocol**:
  1. Mix raw material with appropriate solvent (ethanol/water).
  2. Apply ultrasonic waves (20-100 kHz) to induce cavitation.
  3. Maintain temperature control to prevent thermolabile degradation.
  4. Filter and concentrate the extract.

### Microwave-Assisted Extraction (MAE)
- **Application**: Rapid extraction of polar compounds.

## 2. Pharmaceutical & Nutraceutical Manufacturing Protocols

### Standardized Manufacturing Process (SMP)
- **Granulation**: Wet vs. Dry granulation protocols for tablet consistency.
- **Encapsulation**: Hard shell vs. Softgel manufacturing for nutraceuticals.
- **Coating**: Functional coating protocols for enteric release or taste masking.

### Lyophilization (Freeze-Drying)
- **Application**: Preservation of heat-sensitive pharmaceuticals and nutraceuticals.
- **Protocol**:
  1. **Freezing**: Solidify the product below its triple point.
  2. **Primary Drying (Sublimation)**: Remove ice under vacuum.
  3. **Secondary Drying (Desorption)**: Remove residual bound water.

### Spray Drying
- **Application**: Converting liquid extracts into stable powders.
- **Parameters**: Inlet/outlet temperature control, feed rate, and atomizer speed.

## 3. Food Processing Raw Materials Protocols

### Raw Material Standardization
- **Protocol**:
  1. **Sourcing**: Qualification of suppliers based on GFSI standards.
  2. **Cleaning/Sorting**: Removal of physical contaminants.
  3. **Milling/Size Reduction**: Achieving uniform particle size for processing.
  4. **Stabilization**: Heat treatment (Blanching/Pasteurization) or enzymatic inactivation.

### Ingredient Fractionation
- **Protocol**: Separation of proteins, fibers, and starches from complex food matrices using membrane filtration or centrifugal separation.

## 4. Emerging Technologies
- **Nanotechnology**: Nano-emulsions for enhanced bioavailability of nutraceuticals.
- **Biotechnology**: Fermentation-based production of bioactive ingredients.
- **Continuous Manufacturing**: Transitioning from batch to continuous flow for pharmaceuticals.
